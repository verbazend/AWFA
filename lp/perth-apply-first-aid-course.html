Are there any prerequisites I need to do the course?

The Apply First Aid (formerly known as Senior First Aid) course is competency based training and does not require any previous training or qualifications. Anyone can enrol in our course regardless of your level of experience. We do ask that all students under 14 years must provide written permission from a parent or guardian to attend the course.

What does the certification give me?

Your Statement of Attainment will certify that you have been trained in Apply First Aid HLTFA311A, which encompasses the contents of Perform CPR HLTCPR211A and Provide Basic Emergency Life Support HLTFA211A.

How long does the certification last for?

The Apply First Aid certificate is valid for three (3) years, provided that the Perform CPR component is updated annually. This is in line with the Australian Resuscitation Council guidelines.

Who is Australia Wide First Aid?

Starting out in 1975, Australia Wide First Aid prides itself on serving individuals and corporations in a professional, efficient and friendly manner. Whether you or members of your organisation need to undertake a First Aid Course or you need to purchase a First Aid Kit for your home or office, we have you covered for all your First Aid requirements.

First Aid Course Outline:

Working knowledge of:

  • What are the basic ethics and theory involved in first aid practice
  • What are the measures that need to be taken to effectively deal with issues concerning major or minor injury and illnesses
  • What are the most important management steps that needs to be undertaken when dealing with potentially critical health conditions
  • What is the basic health and safety knowledge required when dealing with the conditions of first aid situations
  • What are the principles, procedures and standard precautions that need to be undertaken to control infection and its spread
  • Master the knowledge of the ‘Chain of survival’
  • What skills should a First Aider possess and what limitations should they be aware of
  • How and when an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) should be used

First Aid management of:

  • Injuries:
    cold and crush injuries; eye and ear injuries; head, neck and spinal injuries; minor skin injuries; needle stick injuries; soft tissue injuries including sprains, strains and dislocations
  • Burns:
    Due to thermal, chemical, friction or electrical contact
  • Environmental Impact:
    Hypothermia, hyperthermia, dehydration, or heat stroke
  • Envenomation:
    Of snake, spider, insect and marine bites
  • Medical conditions:
    Such as, cardiac conditions, epilepsy, diabetes, asthma and other respiratory conditions.
  • Substance Misuse:
    Higher intake of common drugs and alcohol, including illicit drugs
  • Other conditions:
    Including, allergic reactions, bleeding, chest pain, choking/airway obstruction, fractures, near drowning, respiratory distress, seizures, shock, stroke, altered or loss of consciousness, casualty with signs of no life, excessive intake of poisoning and toxic substances (including chemical contamination)
  • Awareness of stress manage techniques and available support

Handling social/legal matters:

  • First aiders duty of care
  • Awareness of what is culturally acceptable
  • Importance of counselling and support
  • Maintaining confidentiality
  • Awareness of your own skills and limitations
